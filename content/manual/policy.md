The rules that make combined prep beat individual prep, without either person dragging the other.

## Tracks
- **Harsha:** DSA → CP → ML depth → ML system design
- **Akanksh:** mental math (daily) → combinatorics → probability → stats/LA → Markov chains & game theory → mocks. Plus DSA as joint backup track (backup roles are pivotal; quant is the lottery ticket).

## Cycles, not lockstep
- Work in **2-week cycles**. Each person sets a checkpoint at cycle start (weekly-cycle template) and self-certifies at the end: done / partial / slipped.
- Slipping is **private**: reschedule inside your own track. Your slippage consumes *your* buffer, never your partner's calendar.

## Buffers
One named **buffer week at each month end** (Aug, Sep, Oct). Priority inside a buffer week: catch-up → revision queue → mocks. Never new topics.

## Collisions (thesis, exams, events)
Declare hard blockers on the shared calendar as soon as known. A blocked week converts to **Reading Week**: only fragment-friendly work (article digests, mental math, flashcards), zero accountability, partner continues normally.

## Junction points (the actual synergy)
| Ritual | Cadence | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Puzzle hour (Akanksh leads) | weekly, 1 h | cheap for Harsha, teaching reps for Akanksh |
| Teaching swap | bi-weekly, 30 min | Feynman effect both ways |
| Cross-domain mock | once per phase | interviewer skill + exposure |
| Weekly card + 30-min sync | weekly | accountability floor |
| Monthly retro | monthly | plan correction |

Banned as fake synergy: joint grinding sessions, lockstep topic pacing, attending each other's deep dives, daily check-ins.

## Scoreboard
"Combined > individual" is measured by: mock scores trending up, roadmap % on schedule, streak coverage, and ultimately **at least one offer in the common-syllabus direction**.
